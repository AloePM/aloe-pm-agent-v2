# Owner Reporting — Aloe Property Management (Lea)

Lea uses this skill to assemble the period's owner update. An owner report is a trust document — the owner reads it to decide whether their property is being run well. The job is an honest picture plus any decision they need to make, right in front of them.

Hard rule: **Lea drafts only.** This skill never sends the report to the owner. Randi reviews and approves before anything goes out.

---

## Step 1 — Gather the Four Pillars

Pull from Rentvine via Bo and Ari for the reporting period (usually a month). If any pillar is missing or looks wrong, name the gap in the draft — never paper over it.

**Work orders (ask Ari):**
- Work orders opened, completed, and still open this period
- Any notable jobs: large, recurring, or requiring owner awareness
- Status of any open work orders carried from prior periods

**Maintenance spend (ask Bo):**
- Total maintenance spend for the period, itemized by job
- Routine spend vs. anything unusual or over the NTE threshold
- Any unpaid vendor bills tied to the property

**Rent collected (ask Bo):**
- Rent billed vs. rent actually collected
- Outstanding or delinquent balance at period end
- Any NSF or payment plan active on the account

**Occupancy (pull from Rentvine):**
- Occupied vs. vacant
- Move-ins and move-outs this period
- Any units in turn and days vacant if a unit sat empty

---

## Step 2 — Write the Plain-Language Summary

The owner is not a property manager. Write the way you would explain it to a smart person who does not live in this world.

- **Lead with the bottom line.** Did the property perform well? One or two sentences up top: rent came in, unit stayed occupied, here is the one thing to watch.
- **Plain words.** "We collected all the rent" beats a collections-rate table with no context. Numbers support the sentence — they do not replace it.
- **Honest about problems.** If a repair ran high, a unit went vacant, or rent came in short, state it plainly with the why. Owners forgive bad news. They do not forgive being surprised by it later.
- **Keep it tight.** A few short sections — performance, maintenance, financial, what needs you — not a wall of data.

---

## Step 3 — Flag What Needs the Owner's Decision

This is the section that earns the report. Pull anything requiring the owner to decide into its own clearly marked section so it cannot be missed.

**NTE threshold:**
- Default authorization limit is **$350** per job unless the Aptly work order specifies a different amount
- Any repair whose cost exceeds the owner's NTE needs explicit approval before proceeding
- Surface the job, the quote, and why it is needed

**Also flag:**
- Capital or replacement items (HVAC, roof, appliance replacement)
- Recurring problems that may need a bigger fix
- Anything time-sensitive where a slow answer costs money or risks a vacancy

For each flagged item include:
- What it is and why it matters
- The cost and Ari's recommendation
- What happens if the owner says yes vs. no vs. waits
- Whether it is time-sensitive

---

## Step 4 — Handoff to Randi

The finished report is a draft for Randi to review. Randi checks the numbers, adjusts the framing if needed, and sends. Lea never goes directly to the owner.

Call out in the handoff:
- Any number that looks off or needs Randi's confirmation
- Any missing data pillar
- Any flagged item that needs Randi's decision before the report goes out

Owner communication goes via email through Aptly or Quo SMS per owner preference.

---

## Output Shape

- **Bottom-line summary** — one or two plain sentences on how the property did
- **Performance section** — occupancy, move-ins/outs, units in turn, days vacant
- **Maintenance section** — work orders opened / completed / open, notable jobs
- **Financial section** — rent billed vs. collected, outstanding balance, maintenance spend
- **Needs-your-decision section** — each over-NTE / capital / recurring item with cost, recommendation, and yes/no/wait consequence
- **Data quality notes** — any missing or suspect pillar, named plainly
- **Handoff status** — draft ready for Randi's review, not sent

---

## Validation

- All four pillars sourced from Rentvine, Bo, or Ari — nothing estimated.
- Any missing pillar is named in the draft, not omitted.
- Every over-NTE item is in the needs-your-decision section with cost and recommendation.
- Report was not sent to the owner — routed to Randi for approval.
- Owner communication channel (email / Quo) noted per owner preference.
